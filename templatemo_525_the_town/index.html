About Me

About Me

Hi there! My name’s Warren. I’m a biomedical engineer and computer engineer from the New York City area. Math and science are more than a passion for me ⁠— they are a way of life. I also enjoy hiking, skiing, music, camping and board games.

I see the human body as an engineering marvel that we can use to inspire new technologies outside of biology. At the same time, I believe we can use computers and electronics alongside biology to augment health care.

I’m an easy-going guy, a hard worker and a good team player, and I love meeting new people. My goal is to find solutions to vital, vexing problems, and I always, always strive for excellence.

Experience & Education

Experience & Education

I work for the U.S. Navy as a civilian computer engineer. In that role, I head a five-person information-assurance team for the U.S. Coast Guard Offshore Patrol Cutter program and serve as a technical expert for the U.S. Army National Guard, where I train site personnel for Command Cyber Readiness Inspections. I am most proud of my work as lead developer for a cybersecurity-automation tool that has already saved the Navy $200,000 a year. In my spare time, I volunteer with various STEM outreach programs and events, such as the Navy's STEM outreach program and the New York Maker Faire.

I graduated from the Rutgers University School of Engineering Honors Program in 2016 with a double major in biomedical engineering and computer engineering. My coursework included:

During college, I worked as a summer research intern for the R&D department of Biomet, Inc., a medical-technology company in Parsippany, NJ, where I collected and analyzed clinical data, among other tasks.

In high school, I focused heavily on math and science, taking all honors science courses, AP calculus and AP statistics. I deeply cherished my experience as a Boy Scout, achieving the rank of Eagle Scout in 2011. Over several summers during college, I worked and volunteered at Resica Falls Scout Camp in East Stroudsburg, PA.

My Projects

CyberKnight

CyberKnight is a cybersecurity automation scanner. It automatically scans systems to determine the operating system environment and detect installed software. It then scans the operating system and software settings and generates a detailed report for a security analyst to review.

CyberKnight is built in two components: an engine and a script repository. The repository contains a separate script for each vulnerability. The engine is the job manager, executing appropriate scripts in parallel and collecting results.The individual vulnerability scripts are written in bash and the engine is implemented with Go.
